Carbonara

Carbonara is a pasta dish made with eggs, hard cheese, fatty cured pork, and black pepper. The cheese is usually pecorino romano. Spaghetti is the most common pasta. while guanciale, a cured pork jowl, is traditional, some variations use pancetta.

Carbonara in Italian means charcoal burner. It is said that this dish got it's name because it was a favorite for these hard workers who needed something tasteful and filling after a day in the mines.

ingredients for 4 people

  • fresh spaghetti 500gr
  • guanciale or pancetta 250gr
  • pecorino or parmesan cheese 150gr
  • 3 eggs
  • fresh black pepper
  • salt

step by step

  1. Bake the pork till crispy
  2. Cook the spaghetti in boiling water with plenty of added salt for about 10 minutes till al dente. Save a cup of the cooking water
  3. Add the spaghetti in the pan with the pork and turn of the heaters
  4. Whip the eggs in a bowl and add the cheese and pepper
  5. Add the egg mixture in the pan with the spaghetti together with the saved water and mix it till a smooth pasta dish
