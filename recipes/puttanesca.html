Spaghetti alla Puttanesca

Spaghetti alla puttanesca is a pasta dish made typically with tomatoes, olive oil, olives, anchovies, chili peppers, capers, and garlic.

The word puttana means prostitute in Italian. Because the dish was first served in the red light district of Rome and served as a nutrious meal with a spicy kick in it it got it's name.

ingredients for 4 people

  • fresh spaghetti 400gr
  • 4 gloves of garlic, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of chili flakes
  • 8 anchovies filets
  • 200gr of Kalamata olives
  • 4 tablespoons of capers
  • 400gr of canned chopped tomatoes
  • 6 tablespoons of extra virgine olive oil
  • salt and pepper

step by step

  1. Put the olive oil, the chili flakes and the garlic in a large pan on low heat for about 5 mins
  2. Add the anchovies to the pan and put the heat on medium
  3. When the anchovies have melted add the olives and capers and bake for 5 mins
  4. Add the tomatoes and let the sauce simmer for 15mins
  5. Cook the spaghetti in boiling water with plenty of added salt for about 10 minutes till al dente. Save a cup of the cooking water
  6. Add the spaghetti to the sauce with the cooking water and mix well
